Distribution fitting

Pearson product-moment correlation analysis was initially performed to test for serial correlation within the bout duration data series (coefficient r; M/F: 0.07/0.16 and p-value>0.16 in both tests). The results confirm that any dependence of bout duration on the duration of the preceding sleep bout in both males and female flies is weak and justifies the futher use of parametric distribution fitting.

Initially the data for both male and female flies was pooled across all 24 hours for parametric fitting. For both male and female Canton S flies, the log-normal distribution was the best fit among the 4 tested distributions. The exponential distribution yielded the worst fit both in males and females (see Fig. 5 ).

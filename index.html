Exploratory analysis of sleep bout durations in Drosophila melanogaster flies

Sleep is a widely conserved feature across animal species, known to be critical for maintaining life. Yet the function and regulation of sleep remain unclear and poorly understood. This project explores the distribution of sleep bouts in a model organism, Drosophila melanogaster, to help elucidate possible functional forms of sleep.

Abstract

Sleep is a well conserved feature pertaining to all animal species, known to be essential for life. However, the function and regulation of sleep are still not well understood. This study presents an exploratory analysis of the data to gain a better understanding of the sleep bout duration distribution by considering a range of parametric distributions with biologically interpretable functional forms. The distributions used in the analysis are exponential, log-normal, power law (Pareto) and stretched exponential (Weibull). Exploratory analysis was performed on the data autogenerated from 850 flies using a novel highthroughput platform designed for activity monitoring of fruit flies, from which 1508845 inactivity bouts were extracted . The results of the distributional analysis were used to inform a parametric survival regression model for sleep bout durations where the effect of covariates, such as fly ID, hour of day and sex, were investigated.

Summary

Sleep to this day remains one of the biggest scientific mysteries. Despite being an essential aspect of animal life and an area of extensive research, regulation of sleep and its functions remain poorly understood. Drosophila flies have been used in studies as a model organism for human sleep due to homologous sleep features. Flies have a fragmented pattern of sleep which generates intervals of sleep of varying lengths, termed bouts. The aim of the study is to present an exploratory analysis of the data to gain a better insight into sleep bout duration distribution in Drosophila flies. Activity tracking data collected from 850 flies was used to generate 1508845 inactivity bouts used for the subsequent analysis. 4 distributions of simple functional forms are fit to the data to gain insight into the regulatory processs underlying sleep. The results of the distribution analysis are used to create a parametric regression survival model for sleep bout durations investigating the effect of covariates, such as ID, sex and time of day.
